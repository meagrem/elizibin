React Native Apps

Words

code

I generally look up and write down every word I come across that I don't know. The "Words" app takes all these words and serializes them out to a React Native app from a Rails server. This app allows for quick and easy lookups of definitions, and also provides a little quiz section as a way for me to memorize and familiarize myself with these words further. Give it a shot!

This app was built in conjunction with a talk I gave on Reactotron at the React-Native Vancouver meetup this autumn. It's built using Expo and uses redux, redux-saga, and react-navigation.

Ruby on Rails Apps

Charcuterie

code

I'm the web developer and a co-editor for the Charcuterie publication.

I've built its initial web presence as a Ruby on Rails application so that I could take advantage of the amazing open-source gem alongslide built by the folks at Triple Canopy to create memorable and unique online reading experiences. Take a look at one of the essays of this publication for an example of how its being used!

Charcuterie strives to provide a forum for experimental writing and informed polemics without pedantry. It assembles a polyphony of inquiry and documents the messy landscape of opinion and critique that unravels in close proximity to where we work, live and make art in Vancouver.

Scan Publication

code

Scan is a contemporary art publication based in Winnipeg, MB run by students at the University of Winnipeg. This Ruby on Rails CMS makes up the online portion of their publication, and allows the students to add issues and essays to the site whenever they please.

It's a very simple CRUD app, but it gets the job done.
